Shiv Parsad lassi Bhandar

A respite for motorists in the hot sun, and a fancy place for the tourists to try something native of the locality, Shiv Prasad Lassi Bhandar is a charmer of sorts. With a backdrop of the mounting Fort that proves contrasting to its unassuming manner, this place serves delicious Lassi (sweet curd topped with thickened cream). A highly popular destination in the city, they serve the lassi in small earthen pots, which undoubtedly provides a cooling effect. There are wooden benches in the outdoor setting, but most of the customers are seen standing in a huddle while relishing.

Ramchandra Chaurasia Tambul Bhandar Banarasi panwala

Ramchandra Chaurasia Tambul Bhandar, often known as Banarasi Panwala, is a well-known establishment in Varanasi, India, famous for its authentic Banarasi paan. Located in one of the oldest cities in the world, Varanasi, this shop has become a cultural icon, attracting both locals and tourists who want to experience the traditional flavors of Banarasi paan.

Kashi chat Bhandar

Kashi Chat Bhandar is particularly famous for its variety of chaats, a popular Indian street food that includes a mix of tangy, spicy, and sweet flavors. Some of the most popular items include Tamatar Chaat (a spicy and tangy tomato-based chaat), Aloo Tikki Chaat (crispy potato patties served with chutneys and yogurt), and Dahi Puri (crispy puris filled with yogurt, chutneys, and spices). Blue Lassi Shop

The Blue Lassi Shop is renowned for its wide variety of lassis, a traditional Indian yogurt-based drink. The lassis here are thick, creamy, and often topped with fresh fruits, nuts, and flavored syrups. Some of the most popular varieties include mango lassi, banana lassi, pomegranate lassi, and mixed fruit lassi. The lassis are served in traditional earthen cups, known as "kulhads," which add an authentic touch to the experience.

Chachi Ki Kachori

"Chachi Ki Kachori" in Varanasi is a well-known local spot famous for its authentic and flavorful kachoris. Varanasi, being one of the oldest cities in India, has a rich culinary tradition, and street food plays a significant role in the local culture. Chachi Ki Kachori in Varanasi is particularly famous for its crispy kachoris stuffed with a spicy filling, usually made of urad dal (split black gram) and a blend of aromatic spices. These kachoris are typically served with a tangy and spicy aloo sabzi (potato curry) and chutney, making for a satisfying and flavorful meal. The kachoris are often enjoyed as a breakfast item or as a snack throughout the day. The stall or shop usually attracts long queues of locals and tourists alike, all eager to taste the authentic flavors that Varanasi's street food is known for. The name "Chachi" adds a touch of nostalgia and warmth, suggesting a homely taste and quality.

Lakshmi Chai Wala

"Lakshmi Chai Wala" in Varanasi is a well-known tea stall, famous for its unique flavors and traditional approach to making tea. Located in the vibrant and culturally rich city of Varanasi, this tea stall has gained popularity among both locals and tourists. Known for serving different varieties of chai (tea), Lakshmi Chai Wala might offer everything from the classic masala chai to unique blends infused with spices that cater to local tastes.

Malaiyo Shop , lassi ,Rabri ,malai and other milk products

Malaiyo (also known as Makhan Malai) is a winter specialty in Varanasi. It's a frothy, creamy dessert made by churning milk and flavored with saffron, cardamom, and sometimes garnished with pistachios and almonds. The process of making Malaiyo involves leaving milk outside overnight to collect dew, which gives it an airy texture.

The Ram Bhandar kachori Jalebi shop

Ram Bhandar has been serving the people of Varanasi for several decades. It is considered one of the oldest and most respected eateries in the city. The shop has maintained its traditional methods of preparation, which has contributed to its enduring popularity. The shop is also known for its fresh jalebis, which are crispy, sweet, and slightly tangy due to the syrup they are soaked in. The combination of hot kachoris followed by sweet jalebis is a favorite breakfast choice for many.
